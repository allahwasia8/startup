## Introduction to Nuneaton and Bedworth Borough Council

<p>Nuneaton and Bedworth Borough Council is the district authority serving the towns of Nuneaton and Bedworth, the village of Bulkington and surrounding communities in northern Warwickshire, england. The council delivers a wide range of public services and manages localized civic services that affect daily life for over 134,000 residents. Nuneaton and Bedworth Borough Council operates as a lower-tier district authority within the two-tier local government system, meaning it handles different responsibilities than Warwickshire County Council.</p><p>The bedworth borough council role covers waste, housing, planning, environmental health and leisure, while Warwickshire County Council manages education, highways, social care and libraries. This distinction matters because contacting the wrong authority can cause unnecessary delays.</p><p>Residents and businesses in nuneaton and bedworth can access council services online through the official website, by phone on 024 7637 6376, or in person at the town hall on coton road, Nuneaton (CV11 5AA). The council also works closely with partner organisations, including housing associations and Warwickshire County Council, to deliver joined-up local support.</p>

## FAQ



### Which council is responsible for my service in Nuneaton or Bedworth?

<p>Warwickshire County Council handles county-level services such as schools, social care, major roads and libraries. Nuneaton and Bedworth Borough Council covers local services like waste collection, housing, environmental health and local planning. If you are unsure, use the postcode search tool on both councils' websites. For example, a pothole on a main road is a County Council matter, while a missed bin collection is an NBBC issue. A solicitor dealing with property or planning queries in the area would also need to distinguish between the two authorities when seeking information.</p>

### How do I register for council housing in Nuneaton and Bedworth?

<p>Create an account on the NBBC Homes website, complete the Housing Options Wizard and submit the required documentation. Eligibility depends on factors including local connection (whether you live, work or have family in the area), housing need, medical circumstances and immigration status. Once registered, you are placed in a band reflecting your level of need. Properties are then advertised for bidding, and offers are made based on banding and date order. Detailed guidance is provided on the NBBC Homes website or by calling the housing team on 02476 376406.</p>

### What should I do if I am at risk of homelessness?

<p>Contact the council's housing options team as early as possible. During office hours, call 02476 376406 or visit the town hall by appointment. For urgent out-of-hours situations, use the emergency contact number published on the NBBC website. The council can assess your situation, help negotiate with landlords or family members, explore financial support options and, where necessary, arrange temporary accommodation in line with statutory duties.</p>

### How can I report a missed bin collection or fly tipping?

<p>Use the online reporting forms on the Nuneaton and Bedworth Borough Council website. Select the correct option - "missed bin" or "fly tipping" - and provide your address, the date of the missed collection or incident, and the type of waste involved. For fly tipping, include the location and any photos you have. Reports are typically acknowledged within a few working days, and the council will arrange collection or investigation as appropriate.</p>

### Where can I find the latest council tax information for Nuneaton and Bedworth?

<p>Up-to-date council tax bands, charges and discounts are published each financial year on the NBBC website. Separate sections cover exemptions, single-person discounts and Council Tax Support for those on low incomes. If you have questions about payments, arrears or changes in circumstances, contact the council tax team using the details on your bill or via the main switchboard on 024 7637 6376.</p>
