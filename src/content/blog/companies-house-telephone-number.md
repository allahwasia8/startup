<p>Whether you need to chase a filing, clarify a deadline, or resolve an issue with your company record, knowing the correct companies house tel number is the first step. Companies House is an executive agency of the UK government responsible for maintaining the register of companies - it registers new limited companies and dissolves them, and holds the public record of company information that anyone can access.</p><p>This guide gives you every contact detail you need, explains when to call, and covers the alternative ways to get in touch so you can handle your business efficiently.</p>

## Key Takeaways

<ul><li><p>The official contact number for Companies House is <strong>0303 1234 500</strong>, suitable for most UK-based enquiries.</p></li><li><p>Contact centre lines are open 8.30am to 6pm, monday to friday, and closed on weekends and UK public holidays.</p></li><li><p>The Companies House main office at Crown Way, Cardiff, CF14 3UZ is not open to the public for walk-in queries - most matters should be handled by telephone, email, or online.</p></li><li><p>You can email Companies House at enquiries@companieshouse.gov.uk for general enquiries.</p></li><li><p>Alternative accessible contact options include a minicom/textphone number (029 2022 6788), a Welsh language contact number (029 2038 0065), and Relay UK for hearing-impaired users.</p></li></ul>

Roaming rates vary, so people calling from outside the uk may find email or online forms more cost-effective.</p><blockquote><p>Before picking up the phone, have your company number, full registered company name, and any relevant filing reference to hand. ## Sending Documents To Companies House By Post

<p>Although online filing is usually faster and provides instant confirmation, some documents may still need to be sent to Companies House by post. All paper filings for companies across the uk should normally be sent to the main office in Cardiff unless official guidance states otherwise.</p><p>Your envelope and covering letter should clearly show:</p><ul><li><p>The full registered company name (exactly as on the register)</p></li><li><p>The company registration number</p></li></ul><p>To request a simple acknowledgement of receipt, enclose a stamped addressed envelope and a copy of your covering letter. Companies House will date stamp the copy and return it. This is confirmation that documents have been received - not that they have been accepted, examined, or registered.</p><blockquote><p>Post well before statutory deadlines. Companies House does not treat postal delays as a valid reason to waive late filing penalties.</p></blockquote>

## Filing Accounts And Other Documents On Time

<p>Timely filing keeps your company in good standing on the public register and avoids penalties. Companies House maintains a public record of company information, meaning late or missing filings are visible to anyone who searches your company.</p><p>Key filing deadlines:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Private companies:</strong> Annual accounts due 9 months after the financial year end</p></li><li><p><strong>Public companies:</strong> Annual accounts due 6 months after the financial year end</p></li><li><p><strong>Confirmation statements:</strong> Due within 14 days of the review period end</p></li></ul><p>Late filing of accounts results in automatic civil penalties, which increase the longer the delay continues. The contact centre can answer general questions about deadlines and filing methods, but cannot grant extensions outside the formal rules.</p><p>Companies House offers an online platform for filing accounts and company updates electronically, which gives instant submission and a clear timestamp. Directors remain legally responsible for meeting deadlines even if they delegate the process to accountants or third-party agents.</p><p>With over 5.44 million active companies on the register, the transformation toward digital filing has become a core part of the agency's strategy for managing volume efficiently.</p>

### Postal Delays And Late Filing Penalties

<p>Relying on last-minute posting can create serious compliance risks. Companies House generally does not accept postal delays, industrial action, or courier issues as valid reasons to cancel late filing penalties.</p><p>Documents are considered delivered when they are received by Companies House - not when they are posted. This is a critical distinction.</p><p><strong>How to protect yourself:</strong></p><ul><li><p>File online wherever possible for instant proof of submission</p></li><li><p>If posting is unavoidable, send documents at least two weeks before the deadline</p></li><li><p>Keep proof of posting and tracking details for your own records</p></li><li><p>Use signed-for or tracked services to verify delivery</p></li></ul>

## Frequently Asked Questions



### Can I visit the Companies House main office in person?

<p>The main Companies House office at crown way, Cardiff is not open to the public for walk-in queries or counter services. Only external postal facilities - such as the 24-hour post box - are available for dropping off documents, and no reception staff will issue a receipt. Most queries should be handled by telephone, email, or online services. If you need to visit a regional office, check the GOV.UK Companies House page for current availability at the Edinburgh, Belfast, or Cardiff offices.</p>

### Is the Companies House telephone number free to call?

<p>The 0303 1234 500 number is not free, but it is charged at the same rate as standard 01 or 02 geographic numbers. Many inclusive call packages from UK landline and mobile providers cover calls to 03 numbers, meaning there may be no additional cost for some callers. If you are unsure about charges, check with your phone provider before calling.</p>

### What should I have ready before calling the Companies House contact centre?

<p>Have your company registration number and full registered company name ready - exactly as it appears on the register. Prepare any relevant dates, such as filing deadlines or the date a document was submitted, to help staff find information quickly. Having access to email during the call can also be useful if staff need to send links to online guidance or forms.</p>

### Can Companies House staff give me legal or tax advice over the phone?

<p>No. Companies House can explain filing requirements, the process for using its services, and how to read the register, but cannot provide legal, accounting, or tax advice. For questions about law, tax, or corporate strategy, seek independent professional advice from a solicitor, accountant, or business adviser. Staff may signpost callers to official guidance or other government bodies where appropriate.</p>

### How quickly will Companies House respond if I contact them by email instead of phone?

<p>Response times vary depending on query types and workload, but Companies House aims to reply to most general enquiries within a few working days. Mark urgent matters clearly in the subject line and include all key details to avoid back-and-forth clarification. For time-critical issues - such as imminent filing deadlines - telephone contact is usually the fastest way to get an answer.</p>
