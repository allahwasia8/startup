## What Is MOT Checking and Why It Matters in 2026

<p>MOT checking means verifying a vehicle's current mot status and mot history online before driving or buying in the UK. An mot test is an annual inspection for road safety and environmental standards, and mot tests help prevent potentially dangerous vehicles from operating on the road. As of 2026, approximately 30 to 35 million tests are carried out every year across Great Britain and Northern Ireland, making online mot history checks a routine part of vehicle ownership.</p><p>A quick free mot check with the vehicle's registration number helps ensure the car is roadworthy, legal, and properly maintained. MOT records are publicly available through the DVSA database for vehicles in England, Scotland, and Wales, while Northern Ireland records are held by the DVA. This information reflects official test results, so you can trust what you see on screen.</p><p>Driving without a valid mot can result in a £1,000 fine, and an expired mot makes your car insurance typically invalid. Whether you're a daily commuter or a buyer browsing classifieds, understanding a vehicle's mot status is no longer optional-it's essential.</p>

## How to Check MOT Status for Free Using a Registration Number

<p>The fastest way to check mot status is online using the vehicle's number plate. You don't need to visit a garage, call anyone, or create an account. Here's how it works:</p><ol><li><p>Visit an official free mot checker or mot check tool online.</p></li><li><p>Enter the vehicle's registration number exactly as it appears on the number plate.</p></li><li><p>View the current mot status, including whether the vehicle has a valid mot certificate and the current mot expiry date.</p></li></ol><p>A free mot check typically shows whether the car currently holds a valid mot, when it runs out, and how many days or months remain. No account, login, or payment details are needed for a basic free mot check-only the correct reg number.</p><p>You can repeat this process for any UK car, van, or motorcycle to confirm it is safe and legal to use on public roads. You can check your mot due date online using the registration number at any time.</p><p>One practical tip: screenshot or note the mot expiry date so you can book your next mot in good time and avoid letting the current mot status lapse. An mot certificate is valid for 12 months from the test date, so setting a calendar reminder is a smart move.</p>

## How to Check a Vehicle's MOT History Online

<p>MOT history checks go beyond the current mot status and show every recorded test, including failures, since records began. By entering the vehicle's registration number into a mot history check tool, users can view test results back to 2005 in Great Britain and 2017 in Northern Ireland. MOT history checks reveal past test results since 2005, giving you a detailed paper trail for any registered vehicle.</p><p>Typical data shown in a full mot history includes:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Test dates</strong> - the test date of every mot since digital records started</p></li><li><p><strong>Pass or fail outcome</strong> - whether previous mot tests resulted in a pass or failure</p></li><li><p><strong>Recorded mileage</strong> - the vehicle's mileage at the time of each inspection</p></li><li><p><strong>Defect notes</strong> - specific reasons for failure, categorised as dangerous defects, major, or minor</p></li><li><p><strong>Advisory notices</strong> - items flagged as approaching a limit but not yet causing failure</p></li><li><p><strong>Test centre</strong> - the name and location of the mot test centre</p></li></ul><p>A mot history check is crucial for buyers to verify a vehicle's condition, recall history, and mileage. MOT history includes recorded mileage at each test, making it easier to verify that odometer readings increase logically over time. If they don't, that's a red flag worth investigating further.</p>

### Using MOT History to Understand Vehicle Condition

<p>A long record of clean passes with few advisory notes suggests careful ownership, regular servicing, and lower risk of sudden, expensive repairs. MOT history checks help identify recurring vehicle issues that might otherwise go unnoticed.</p><p>Here's what to look for when reviewing a car's mot history:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Repeated advisories</strong> - if the same defect (for example, "offside front tyre worn close to legal limit") appears across several mot test results, the owner may be neglecting that component.</p></li><li><p><strong>Frequent failures</strong> - multiple failures over consecutive years or many major defects in past results may indicate poor maintenance, poor repairs, or underlying mechanical issues.</p></li><li><p><strong>Mileage consistency</strong> - compare the mileage recorded each year in the mot history to service invoices, sales adverts, or digital service records to confirm they align.</p></li><li><p><strong>Mileage drops</strong> - any sudden drop in recorded mileage between tests, or unusually small increases for the time elapsed, should be treated as a warning of potential odometer tampering.</p></li></ul><p>Combining the vehicle's mot history with its maintenance history gives the most complete picture of how a car has been treated over its life.</p>

## When Your First MOT Is Due and How Often to Check

<p>In Great Britain, the mot is required on the third anniversary of registration and annually thereafter. For example, a car registered on 15 June 2023 needs its first mot by 15 June 2026. Most vehicles require an mot every 12 months after that initial test.</p><p>In Northern Ireland, the first mot is usually due at four years old, then every year after that. MOT history checks there cover results from 2017 onwards, so newer vehicles may have limited digital records.</p><p>Here are some timing tips:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Check a few times per year</strong> - don't wait until close to expiry. Running a quick check of the vehicle's mot status online helps you plan maintenance and avoid last-minute surprises.</p></li><li><p><strong>Book early</strong> - you can retest a vehicle up to one month before expiry without losing any remaining time on the certificate. This is a useful buffer if repairs are needed.</p></li><li><p><strong>Historic vehicles</strong> - vehicles over 40 years old may be exempt from mot, but owners should still verify definitions, check guidance, and keep the car roadworthy.</p></li></ul><p>Knowing when your mot is due and acting early is the simplest way to stay legal and avoid unnecessary stress.</p>

### What Happens After an MOT Failure

<p>If a car fails its mot, the tester issues a VT30 refusal certificate listing all the mot defects and whether they are dangerous or major. This document becomes part of the vehicle's permanent mot test records.</p><p>Key points to understand after a failure:</p><ul><li><p>Cars with dangerous defects must not be driven on the road until repairs are completed and the vehicle passes a retest. You can only drive to a pre-booked mot test if expired, and only in very limited circumstances allowed by law.</p></li><li><p>You cannot drive a vehicle with an expired mot certificate on public roads. Fines for driving with dangerous defects can reach £2,500, and you may also face penalty points.</p></li><li><p>Many test centres offer a free or reduced-fee retest if the car is repaired and presented again within a set number of working days (often 10 at the same test centre), but drivers should confirm the exact policy with the garage.</p></li><li><p>The maximum government-capped fee for a standard car mot is £54.85, so retesting at the same test centre can save money.</p></li></ul><p>Keep repair invoices and the new mot certificate together so that future buyers can see that failures were properly addressed and that the car now has a clean mot history. The failed test, the reasons for failure, and the eventual pass will all appear in future mot history checks against the same registration number.</p>

## Frequently Asked Questions



### Is an online MOT check really free?

<p>Yes. Basic mot status and mot history checks in the UK are free, providing current mot status, expiry date, past test results, and recorded mileage with no payment required. Some companies offer optional paid reports that bundle mot data with extra checks such as finance, write-off status, or previous damage, but the mot portion itself remains free. Before paying for any upgrade, use the free mot history first to see if you actually need more detailed information.</p>

### Can I check MOT history without knowing the vehicle's registration number?

<p>You cannot access UK mot history without the vehicle's registration number, as this is the unique identifier used in the DVSA and DVA databases. If the number plate is not shown in an advert, request it from the seller before travelling so you can run a mot history check in advance. Make sure to type the registration exactly as displayed on the plate, including any spaces, to avoid errors or missing records.</p>

### Why doesn't my MOT history appear online?

<p>Vehicles under three years old in Great Britain or under four in Northern Ireland may not yet have any mot history because they have not reached their first test date. MOT tests carried out before 2005 in Great Britain and before 2017 in Northern Ireland are not recorded in the current digital mot history system. Also check for typing mistakes in the registration number, and confirm that the vehicle is not mot-exempt (for example, some vehicles more than 40 years old).</p>

### Does an MOT check prove a car has been serviced?

<p>MOT history and service history are separate. An mot check shows legal roadworthiness test results, not full maintenance records. While advisory notices may sometimes hint at overdue future maintenance, buyers should still ask for service book stamps, invoices, or digital service logs to confirm regular servicing. Combining a free mot history check with a review of service paperwork gives a more complete picture of a car's past care.</p>

### Will a clean MOT history guarantee a trouble-free car?

<p>Even a perfect mot history cannot guarantee that a vehicle will never develop faults, as the test is a snapshot of condition on the test date, not a full mechanical inspection. Arrange an independent inspection or test drive, especially for older or higher-mileage vehicles, and use mot history as a starting point rather than the only check. MOT checks, service records, and professional inspections together offer the strongest protection when buying a used car. A five-minute free mot check today can save you hundreds in fines, repair costs, and buyer's regret-so make it part of your routine whenever your mot runs close to expiry or you're considering a purchase.</p>
