<p>Lost your passport with a trip around the corner? A damaged passport discovered the night before packing? These situations happen more often than you'd think, and the UK Government's 1 week fast track service exists precisely for moments like these. This guide walks you through everything you need to know about the passport replacement fast track process - from eligibility and cost to step-by-step booking and what to bring to your appointment.</p>

## Key Takeaways

<ul><li><p>The 1 week fast track service is the main way to urgently replace a lost, stolen or damaged UK passport, with your new passport delivered by courier around one week after your appointment.</p></li><li><p>The 1 week fast track service is for adults and children - covering renewals, replacements, and first child passport applications. You cannot use fast track services for first-time adult passports.</p></li><li><p>You cannot apply for fast track services if outside the UK. You must book an appointment at a passport office and attend in person (or send a representative).</p></li><li><p>The 1 week fast track service costs £192 for an adult passport as of 2026, with higher fees for frequent traveller passports. These are in addition to photo costs.</p></li><li><p>Do not book travel until your new passport physically arrives. The one-week timeframe is a target, not a guarantee.</p></li></ul>

## What Is Passport Replacement Fast Track?

<p>Passport replacement fast track refers to the UK Government's urgent 1 week fast track service, run by HM Passport Office. It lets you get a new passport significantly faster than the standard service when time is short.</p><p>To apply for a replacement passport in the UK, you can use the standard or urgent service. The fast track option exists for situations where three weeks is too long to wait. Here's what it covers:</p><ul><li><p>Renewing an expired or expiring adult passport</p></li><li><p>Replacing a lost, stolen or damaged passport for an adult or child</p></li><li><p>Changing personal details such as name, gender or place of birth</p></li><li><p>Issuing a first child passport</p></li></ul><p>The standard service - where you apply online and wait for postal delivery - typically takes around three weeks in 2026. The 1 day Premium service is the fastest option but is only for renewing adult passports. The 1 week fast track sits in between: faster than standard, broader eligibility than Premium, and always requires a pre-booked appointment at a Passport Customer Service Centre.</p><p>Replacing a lost passport requires immediate action to cancel the old document, so if yours has gone missing, don't wait.</p>

## Who Can Use the 1 Week Fast Track Service?

<p>Not everyone qualifies for the fast track service. The online application process will tell you whether you meet the eligibility criteria, but here's a clear breakdown.</p><p><strong>You can use 1 week fast track if you are:</strong></p><ul><li><p>An adult (aged 16+) renewing a passport - for example, an adult aged 28 with a damaged passport can use fast track</p></li><li><p>An adult or child replacing a lost, stolen or damaged passport</p></li><li><p>Changing personal details on an adult or child passport</p></li><li><p>Applying for a first child passport (under 16)</p></li></ul><p><strong>You cannot use fast track if you are:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Applying for a first adult passport (someone aged 16+ who has never held a British passport)</p></li><li><p>Outside the UK - you must be physically present to attend a passport office appointment</p></li><li><p>Trying to upgrade or speed up a live standard passport application already in progress</p></li></ul><p>You may need a countersignature for a new child passport, when replacing a severely damaged passport, or if your appearance has changed significantly. Check HM Passport Office guidance before booking so you aren't turned away at the appointment.</p>

## Step-by-Step: How to Book and Use Passport Replacement Fast Track

<p>To fast-track a passport replacement in the UK, you need to book an urgent appointment. Here's exactly how the process works, from start to finish.</p><p><strong>Step 1: Check current processing times</strong></p><p>Before paying the premium fee, visit GOV.UK to see how long it's currently taking to get a passport through the standard service. If you have three weeks or more before travel, the cheaper route may be sufficient. You only need a passport urgently if your travel date is close.</p><p><strong>Step 2: Start the application</strong></p><p>Go to the official "Get a passport urgently" page on GOV.UK. You'll answer eligibility questions - passport type, whether it's lost or stolen, whether you're in the UK - and select the 1 week fast track option.</p><p><strong>Step 3: Book an appointment</strong></p><p>Choose a Passport Customer Service Centre. The main locations include London, Liverpool, Glasgow, Belfast, Newport, Peterborough and Durham. The earliest available appointment is often the next day, though availability varies by location and season.</p><p><strong>Step 4: Complete the application form and pay</strong></p><p>Fill in the application form details online. Upload or confirm a compliant digital photo. Pay the fee using a debit card or credit card. You'll receive a confirmation email with your appointment details and reference number.</p><p><strong>Step 5: Attend the appointment</strong></p><p>You must book an appointment at a passport office for fast-tracking. The appointment usually lasts around 10 minutes. Bring your old passport if you still have it, your appointment confirmation, any supporting documents, a completed paper application if required, and a countersignature page where needed. You must bring two identical printed passport photos to your appointment.</p><p>A friend or family member can attend on your behalf if they bring your application reference (starting with "PEX"), your old passport, all requested documents, and a signed and dated letter giving them permission. Their own identity documents (such as a driving licence) will also be checked.</p><p><strong>Step 6: Receive your passport</strong></p><p>The 1 week fast track service delivers passports in 1 week. Your new passport is sent by secure courier, and someone must be at the delivery address to sign for it. Supporting documents may be returned separately via post.</p><p><strong>Step 7: Don't book travel yet</strong></p><p>One week is a target, not a guarantee. Extra checks, missing documents, or high demand can push delivery beyond seven days. ## Documents and Checks Needed for a Fast Track Replacement

<p>Preparing your documents before the appointment is the single biggest thing you can do to avoid delays. Use this as a checklist:</p><p><strong>Core documents for every application:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Your current passport or old passport (if not lost or stolen)</p></li><li><p>Completed application form (online or paper, depending on service type)</p></li><li><p>A compliant digital photo or printed passport photos meeting UK requirements</p></li><li><p>Proof of any personal detail changes - for example, a marriage certificate for a name change, a gender recognition certificate, or a deed poll</p></li></ul><p><strong>For lost or stolen passports:</strong></p><ul><li><p>You can report a lost or stolen passport online via GOV.UK</p></li><li><p>Complete the relevant lost/stolen section on the passport application</p></li><li><p>A police crime reference number is required if your passport was stolen</p></li><li><p>You may face additional security questions at the appointment</p></li></ul><p><strong>For child passport applications:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Full birth certificate</p></li><li><p>Proof of parental responsibility</p></li><li><p>Evidence of British nationality if the child was born abroad</p></li><li><p>Countersignature from an eligible person</p></li></ul><p>HM Passport Office will carry out a digital check against government records. If records don't match, or if your photo looks significantly different from the one on your previous passport, a paper check or even an interview may be required - and this can extend processing times beyond one week.</p>

## 1 Week Fast Track vs 1 Day Premium: Which Is Right for Passport Replacement?

<p>Both services are urgent, but they serve different situations. Here's how to decide.</p><ul><li><p>The 1 day Premium service is only for renewing adult passports. It cannot be used for a first adult passport, child passports, or most lost or stolen cases. The 1 day Premium service requires passports issued after December 31, 2001.</p></li><li><p>The 1 week fast track service is the correct option when you need to replace a lost, stolen or damaged passport, apply for a first child passport, or change details quickly. It covers adult and child passports.</p></li><li><p>The 1 day Premium service issues passports in 4 hours - you collect your passport ready at the office the same day. The earliest appointment for 1 day Premium is 2 days after applying. Fast track delivers by courier about one week after the appointment.</p></li><li><p>The 1 day Premium service costs £207.50 for an adult passport (standard 34-page), rising to £239.50 for a frequent traveller passport. The fast track service is cheaper at around £192 for a standard adult passport.</p></li></ul><blockquote><p>If your flight is in two days and you qualify, Premium is your only realistic option. If you have a week or more, fast track saves you money and covers a wider range of scenarios.</p></blockquote>

## What If You Can't Attend or Need to Change a Fast Track Appointment?

<p>Life doesn't always cooperate with appointment schedules. Here's what you can do:</p><ul><li><p>You can usually change the appointment online using the link in your confirmation email. Changes made more than 48 hours in advance are normally free.</p></li><li><p>Cancelling within 48 hours typically triggers an administration fee of around £34.50, deducted from any refund. Missing the appointment entirely may mean losing the full fee.</p></li><li><p>A trusted person - a friend or family member - can attend your 1 week fast track appointment instead. They must bring your application reference (starting with "PEX"), your documents, their own identity proof, and a dated letter signed by you giving them permission.</p></li><li><p>If you fail to bring the required documents, the appointment may be rescheduled, and your application could be processed as a standard service instead.</p></li></ul><p>Read your confirmation email carefully. HM Passport Office can update these rules, and text messages or emails may contain the latest instructions.</p>

## Alternatives If You're Not Eligible for Fast Track

<p>If you can't use the fast track service, you still have options.</p><ul><li><p><strong>Standard service:</strong> Anyone can apply online for a new or renewed passport. Processing typically takes around three weeks in 2026 if there are no complications. This is the cheapest route.</p></li><li><p><strong>Paper application via Post Office:</strong> If you can't complete the online process, visit a post office for a paper application form. The Check &amp; Send service (around £16 extra) helps catch errors but is usually slower overall.</p></li><li><p><strong>Emergency travel document:</strong> If you're outside the UK and your passport is lost or stolen, you will usually need to apply for an emergency travel document through the nearest British embassy or consulate. You must report a stolen passport to local police if abroad before applying.</p></li><li><p><strong>Passport Adviceline:</strong> For compassionate or medical travel emergencies, call 0300 222 0000. ## Frequently Asked Questions about Passport Replacement Fast Track



### Can I upgrade an existing standard passport application to fast track?

<p>If you already have a standard passport application in progress, you usually cannot simply upgrade it to 1 week fast track online. Booking a completely new urgent appointment while another application is open can lead to extra admin fees and does not always speed things up.</p><p>If travel is within about two weeks, call the Passport Adviceline. HM Passport Office can sometimes offer an upgrade or alternative urgent solution for an extra fee, depending on your eligibility and circumstances. Get advice before taking action to avoid paying twice.</p>

### What happens if my fast track passport doesn't arrive within 1 week?

<p>One week is a target, not an absolute guarantee. Extra security checks, missing documents, or busy periods can extend processing times. Track your passport application online using your reference number, and contact the Passport Adviceline if your passport has not arrived within the expected window.</p><p>Do not book non-refundable travel until you physically have your new passport. Airlines and insurers rarely pay out for trips missed due to passport delays, so treat the delivery date as an estimate rather than a promise.</p>

### Do I still need a countersignature for a fast track replacement?

<p>Many adult passport renewals and straightforward replacements no longer need a countersignature, especially if HM Passport Office can identify you from your previous passport photo. However, a countersignature is more likely required for first child passports, for applicants whose appearance has significantly changed, or where a passport is badly damaged or has been lost or stolen.</p><p>Check HM Passport Office guidance for the most recent list of acceptable countersignatories. Doctors and passport office staff are usually excluded unless special rules apply.</p>

### Can I apply for fast track in Welsh?

<p>It is possible to apply urgently in Welsh, but you should contact HM Passport Office specifically to arrange a Welsh-language fast track service. Welsh forms, guidance and customer support are available, though appointment availability may differ by location and demand. Allow extra time to secure your preferred language service, especially in peak travel seasons.</p>

### Is my old passport returned after a fast track replacement?

<p>For renewals and some replacements, HM Passport Office usually cancels and returns the old passport separately, often with the corners clipped so it cannot be used for travel. If a passport was reported lost or stolen, you should not expect it to be returned. If it later turns up, it must not be used and should be destroyed or sent back to HM Passport Office.</p><p>Once a new passport is issued, older passports with remaining validity can no longer be used for travel - even if they look "in date." Your new passport is the only valid document from that point forward.</p>
