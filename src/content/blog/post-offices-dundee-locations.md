## Frequently Asked Questions about Post Offices in Dundee



### What is the difference between a Post Office branch and a Royal Mail delivery office in Dundee?

<p>A post office branch such as 30 Whitehall Street offers full counter services to the public: posting parcels, financial services, travel money, document certification and more. Royal mail delivery offices at Ward Road and Ivory Place focus on sorting mail and providing collection points for missed deliveries. You cannot pay household bills or access banking at a delivery office.</p>

### Where do I collect a missed Royal Mail delivery in Dundee?

<p>The "Something for You" card left by the royal mail postie will specify whether to collect from Dundee Central Delivery Office at Ward Road, DD1 5ZZ, or from Dundee East Delivery Office at Ivory Place, DD5 3RY. Bring the card and a suitable form of photo ID, and check the printed opening times before travelling.</p>

### Are Dundee post offices open on Sundays?

<p>Most traditional post office branches in Dundee, including Whitehall Street, do not open on Sundays. Some smaller in-store counters within supermarkets or convenience stores may have limited Sunday hours. Always confirm specific Sunday availability online or by phone before setting out.</p>

### Can I exchange foreign currency at a Dundee Post Office?

<p>Selected Dundee branches offer bureau de change services for popular currencies, with less common currencies available to order in advance. There is also a dedicated bureau de change at Happyhillock Shopping Centre, Unit 7–12, Happyhillock Road, DD4 8LS. Check rates and stock before visiting.</p>

### How do I find the nearest Post Office branch to my Dundee address?

<p>Use the official Post Office branch finder online and enter your Dundee postcode - such as DD1, DD3 or DD5 - to see nearby locations, services and opening hours. Compare a couple of nearby branches, as services like passport applications, banking or travel money may only be available at larger Dundee sites. This saves you an unnecessary trip to a branch that does not carry the service you need.</p>
