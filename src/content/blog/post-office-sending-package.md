## How to Send a Package from a Post Office Branch

<p>Whether you need to post a small letter, a 1kg parcel across the UK, or a 10kg box overseas, the process at a post office follows the same core steps. This handy guide walks through everything from preparation at home to handing your item over at the counter in June 2026.</p><p>Here's the order:</p><ol><li><p>Check what you can post</p></li><li><p>Pick and pack your box properly</p></li><li><p>Address and label it clearly</p></li><li><p>Weigh, measure, and choose a service</p></li><li><p>Buy postage (online or in branch)</p></li><li><p>Hand it over at the counter or kiosk</p></li></ol><p>Post Office staff can guide you if you're unsure which royal mail or parcelforce worldwide option will suit your needs.</p>

## Check You Can Post the Item

<p>Some everyday goods are prohibited or restricted when sending items with royal mail and parcelforce. Common examples include aerosols, perfume, nail varnish, gel polish, and power banks or devices containing lithium batteries.</p><p>Before you buy postage, check the current Royal Mail guidance on prohibited and restricted items. At the post office counter, staff will ask what's inside your parcel for safety reasons. Sending prohibited items can lead to delays, returns, or destruction of the contents, and may void any compensation cover entirely.</p>

## Pick and Pack Your Box Properly

<p>Good packaging is key to ensuring letters and parcels arrive safely and qualify for the correct price band.</p><ul><li><p>Use sturdy packaging to secure items during delivery. Choose a box or padded envelope slightly larger than the contents so there's room for cushioning rather than squeezing everything in. Use a box that fits snugly around the item without excessive empty space.</p></li><li><p>Use strong, undamaged boxes. Reused boxes must be free from damage, especially at corners and folds. Remove or cover any old barcodes and delivery labels.</p></li><li><p>Use bubble wrap for delicate or breakable items. Scrunched newspaper or paper works well for general cushioning. Seal every opening with strong parcel tape.</p></li><li><p>For bulky or heavy parcels, parcelforce worldwide services may be the better option when dropped at a post office branch.</p></li></ul>

## Address and Label Your Package Clearly

<p>Clear, correct addressing helps royal mail and parcelforce deliver quickly and avoid returns. Address the package clearly with recipient details and a return address.</p><ul><li><p><strong>UK address format</strong>: house number, street name, town or city, and full postcode. Write in clear, waterproof ink that won't smear.</p></li><li><p><strong>Placement</strong>: the delivery address goes on the largest, flattest face of the parcel - not wrapped around corners or hidden under tape.</p></li><li><p><strong>Return address</strong>: add it on the opposite side or in the top-left corner so undeliverable parcels can be sent back.</p></li><li><p><strong>International mail</strong>: you must include the full address and country in English. Write the destination country name in English capitals on the last line (e.g. "FRANCE", "CANADA") without abbreviations. Address parcels clearly, using capital letters for countries.</p></li></ul>

## UK Delivery Options: Royal Mail and Parcelforce

<p>Royal Mail offers standard post and various courier services for domestic UK letters and parcels. Royal Mail delivers letters, cards, and lightweight parcels reliably. Royal Mail delivered approximately 2.2 billion parcels last year - and handled approximately 2.2 billion parcels across their network.</p><p>Key domestic options include:</p><ul><li><p><strong>1st Class / 2nd Class</strong>: affordable for small and medium parcels; delivery typically within one to two working day windows.</p></li><li><p><strong>Signed For</strong>: adds a signature requirement on delivery for extra security.</p></li><li><p><strong>Tracked 24 / Tracked 48</strong>: full tracking with next-day or two-day delivery targets.</p></li><li><p><strong>Special Delivery Guaranteed by 1pm</strong>: next-day guarantee with signature, photo proof, and up to £750 compensation. ## International Letters and Parcels

<p>Post Office branches can help you send parcels from the UK to destinations worldwide with royal mail and parcelforce worldwide. Royal Mail offers international delivery services for parcels, including tracked, signed, and economy options for smaller items. Royal Mail's International Tracking offers different tracking levels depending on the service chosen.</p><p>For heavier parcels or urgent shipments, parcelforce worldwide provides global priority and express services. Parcelforce Worldwide's globalpriority and globalexpress services are fully tracked from collection to delivery at the destination.</p><p>Custom forms are mandatory for international items sent from the UK. For most Royal Mail items, a CN22 form covers lower-value shipments, while a CN23 applies above that threshold. Custom forms are mandatory for international gifts over £270. Parcelforce uses CP72 forms for international parcels. Incomplete or incorrect declarations can cause delays, returns, or charges at the other end.</p>

## Tracking, Proof of Posting, and Compensation

<p>Select services including tracking or signature for security when sending valuable or time-sensitive items. Royal Mail tracking requires a service with a tracking number - basic 1st and 2nd class without barcodes only offer a certificate of posting as proof.</p><p>Tracked and premium services provide a reference number on the receipt or via the app, which you can enter on the carrier's website to browse and follow progress. As of April 2026, Parcelforce caps compensation at £7,500 per consignment, while Special Delivery offers options up to £10,000 for consequential loss.</p><p>Keep your receipt and tracking number - you'll need them to manage any claims.</p>

## FAQ



### Do I have to go to a Post Office branch to send a parcel with Royal Mail?

<p>Not always. Many barcoded Royal Mail parcels can be posted in parcel postboxes or collected from home if bought online. However, using a post office branch is recommended when you need proof of posting, advice on the range of services, or need to send Special Delivery and some international items that require customs forms.</p>

### Can I send a package abroad without filling in a customs form?

<p>For almost all gifts and goods sent from the UK to countries outside the UK, a customs form (CN22, CN23, or CP72 for parcelforce worldwide) is legally required. Sending without one can cause delays, returns, or unexpected charges at the destination. Approximately 2.2 billion parcels were delivered last year by Royal Mail, and smooth customs documentation is what keeps international mail moving.</p>

### What happens if my parcel is too big or heavy for standard Royal Mail services?

<p>Parcels above Royal Mail's maximum size or weight limits (20kg, 61 × 46 × 46 cm for medium parcels) must be sent via parcelforce worldwide, which can handle larger items up to around 30kg and greater dimensions. These can still be arranged and handed over at a post office branch.</p>

### Can I send multiple parcels quickly without queuing every time?

<p>Regular senders and marketplace sellers can use fast-drop solutions such as Drop &amp; Go or pre-paid online labels. This lets you hand over several parcels at once at the counter or designated drop area, with receipts and tracking provided in one go - a quick and easy way to manage high-volume sending.</p>

### Is my parcel insured automatically when I post it?

<p>Most Royal Mail and Parcelforce products include a basic level of inclusive compensation cover. However, higher-value goods - like shoes, electronics, or jewellery - may need a premium service or extra cover added at the post office counter to ensure adequate protection against loss or damage.</p>
