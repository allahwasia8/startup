MOT reminders: how to set them up, manage them, and never miss your test

Forgetting your MOT date is easier than most people think. Research suggests that 28% of cars are overdue for MOTs at any time, largely due to forgetfulness. The good news is that setting up free mot reminders takes a moment, costs nothing, and can save you from fines, voided insurance, and unsafe driving. This guide walks you through everything you need to know - from signing up to managing alerts for a single car or an entire fleet.

Key Takeaways

  • Drivers can get free mot reminders by entering their vehicle's registration number and an email address or mobile phone number on the gov.uk website.

  • Reminders are usually sent several weeks before the mot due date, giving you time to book a test and fix any issues in advance.

  • Driving without a valid mot can lead to fines up to £1,000 and may invalidate your insurance - and there is no grace period after an mot expiration.

  • Motorists can view, change, or stop their mot reminders online at any time if they sell the car, scrap it, or no longer need alerts.

  • Checking mot status and outstanding safety recalls with a registration number helps keep every vehicle safe and legally roadworthy.

What is an MOT reminder and why it matters

An mot reminder is a free notification sent by email or text message before a vehicle's mot is due. The DVSA provides an mot reminder service in the UK, designed to ensure that keepers never accidentally let their mot certificate lapse. A reminder ensures you book an appointment before your current mot certificate expires, rather than discovering the problem after it is too late.

Reminders reduce the risk of forgetting a date that was set a full year ago. Most vehicles need an mot every 12 months after three years of age, and a single busy week is all it takes to miss the deadline. Because 28% of cars are overdue for MOTs due to forgetfulness, automated alerts solve a widespread problem with almost no effort.

A current mot is a legal requirement for most vehicles over three years old in Great Britain, with only limited exemptions for historic vehicles and certain other categories. Missing it carries real consequences:

  • Fines of up to £1,000 for driving without a valid mot, rising to £2,500 if the vehicle is in a dangerous condition.

  • Insurance invalidation - most policies require a valid mot, and claims can be refused without one.

  • Possible seizure of the vehicle by police, especially if it is found to be dangerously defective on the road.

Reminders are available for cars, vans, and motorcycles that are recorded on the dvla database.

How to set up a free MOT reminder

Setting up free mot reminders is straightforward. MOT reminders can be set up via the gov.uk website, and the whole process takes less than two minutes. Here is how to request one.

  1. Navigate to the official mot reminders page on GOV.UK. You can sign up for the GOV.UK MOT Reminder Service using your vehicle's registration number. Enter the registration exactly as it appears on the number plate.

  2. The system will pull your vehicle details from dvla records - make, model, colour, and year of first registration. Confirm these are correct before continuing. If anything looks wrong, double-check the registration number you entered.

  3. Choose how you want to receive alerts. You can receive mot reminders via text or email, or both. Provide a valid email address, a mobile phone number, or both contact methods.

  4. The service checks the current mot status and calculates the next due date before activating reminders. If the vehicle already has a valid mot, the system automatically schedules notifications based on the expiry date.

  5. You will usually receive a confirmation message to confirm that reminders have been activated. You can repeat the process for more than one vehicle if needed - each registration is handled separately.

When MOT reminders are sent and how they work

Reminder schedules are fixed to give plenty of notice before the test is due. The first notification from the mot reminder service is sent four weeks before the test is due. Additionally, owners may receive a letter by post about seven weeks before the mot anniversary, ensuring that even those without email or sms signup get a warning.

A typical pattern from private services looks like this: reminders sent around 60 days, 30 days, 14 days, and 7 days before the mot expiry date. Timings differ slightly between the official government reminders and private services, but they always use the vehicle's mot due date from dvla records.

If the driver has an early test and the new mot is recorded, the reminder schedule automatically updates to the new expiry date. There is no need to manually reset anything - the system recalculates based on the latest data.

Reminders are usually sent between standard UK business hours and may not arrive at the exact same time for every vehicle.

How MOT reminders help you plan ahead

Use the first reminder (around 60 days out) to compare garage prices, check opening times, and confirm availability. This early alert is your chance to shop around rather than accept whatever is available at the last moment.

The second reminder (around 30 days out) is a good time to book a test and schedule any known repairs or servicing - worn tyres, a cracked windscreen, or a failed bulb. Getting these sorted before the test saves time and avoids a fail.

The final reminder (around 7 days out) should be treated as a last warning. If the car is not booked in or already tested by this point, act immediately.

Combine mot reminders with calendar entries on your phone or work diary for extra peace of mind. Fleet operators can use multiple reminders to spread bookings across the month and avoid all vehicles being off the road at once.

How to view, update, or stop your MOT reminders

Drivers remain in control and can manage or cancel their mot reminder subscription at any time. The process is simple and available online.

To view reminder details, visit the reminders page and enter the vehicle's registration number along with the email address or mobile number used at signup. The system will display your current reminder settings.

If you change phone number or prefer to switch from text to email reminders, update your contact details through the same page. Failing to update after changing your mobile or email provider means alerts may never reach you.

To stop reminders - for example after selling, scrapping, or exporting the vehicle, or declaring it off the road with a SORN - follow the cancellation instructions on the service page. Stopping reminders does not cancel or extend an mot certificate; it only stops future notifications from being sent.

When you should stop reminders for a vehicle

Cancel reminders in these specific situations:

  • Vehicle sold to a new keeper

  • Car written off or scrapped

  • Vehicle exported abroad

  • Vehicle permanently declared SORN

Company car drivers should ask their fleet manager before cancelling reminders on a vehicle they do not own personally. Failing to stop reminders after a sale may result in messages going to the wrong person, causing confusion for the new owner.

After buying a used car, the new keeper should set up fresh mot reminders using the registration number and their own email address. Cancelling reminders does not remove the vehicle from the dvla database - mot status and history can still be checked online.

Other ways to unsubscribe from MOT reminders

Most email reminders include a clearly marked "unsubscribe" or "manage preferences" link at the bottom of the message. Click it to stop or adjust your alerts.

Text message reminders may allow you to stop alerts by replying with a keyword such as "STOP" or following a short link in the sms. Unsubscribing by sms may cost the price of a standard network text, depending on your mobile tariff.

Keep at least one reminder active until the vehicle is definitely sold or no longer on the road, to avoid accidental lapses in mot cover. If you delete the original message and cannot find the unsubscribe details, you can usually manage reminders online using your registration number.

Checking your MOT status and safety recalls

Before relying on any reminder, every driver should know how to check their vehicle's mot status independently. Use the vehicle's registration number on the official mot status check to see whether there is a valid certificate, the expiry date, and any advisory notes.

The mot history page usually lists previous tests, including passes, fails, and advisories such as worn tyres, steering wear, or brake issues. This data is publicly available and free to access through the dvla vehicle enquiry service.

Reminders also indicate if a vehicle has outstanding safety recalls issued by the manufacturer. Act quickly on recalls and serious advisories, as these can affect both safety and future mot results. Ignoring advisories is surprisingly common - recent RAC surveys found that around 44% of drivers who received advisory notices failed to act on all of them.

Some services also let you check vehicle tax, road tax renewal status, and insurance details alongside mot documents in one view.

What if the MOT details look wrong?

Compare the online mot expiry date with the date printed on your most recent mot certificate. If they do not match, contact the testing station that carried out the test to check whether results were submitted correctly.

Persistent discrepancies can be raised with the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) for investigation. Mot reminders can only be as accurate as the data stored in the dvla and DVSA systems, so keeping records up to date is essential. Follow any official guidance from the DVSA if you suspect an error.

Keep digital or paper copies of mot certificates and major repair invoices in case proof is needed later. These documents protect you if there is ever a dispute about your vehicle's test history.

Owners of more than one vehicle - families, tradespeople, or small fleets - benefit especially from a structured mot reminder service. Tracking due dates across several vehicles without a system is a recipe for missed tests and parked vans.

Fleet-oriented services can track every vehicle's mot due date in one dashboard using each vehicle's registration number. Missed MOTs in a fleet can lead to parked vans, lost jobs, and higher compliance risks, so centralised reminders are vital.

Choosing a useful MOT reminder service for a fleet

Fleet operators should look for services that pull mot data directly from the dvla vehicle enquiry service to minimise manual errors. Prioritise email reminders backed up by dashboard views, with optional sms reminders for critical vehicles.

Assign reminders to both primary and backup contacts so that messages are not lost if someone is on leave. A clear compliance calendar showing which vehicles are due in each week can help plan around busy trading periods and avoid fuel-wasting emergency trips to distant garages.

Trial the service with a small number of vehicles first, then roll it out across the fleet once reliability is proven.

Legal and safety consequences of missing your MOT

Driving without a valid mot - where one is legally required - is illegal in Great Britain. You can only drive without an mot to a pre-booked test or from a test site to get repairs done. Any other use of the road is an offence.

Police and enforcement agencies use automatic number plate recognition (ANPR) cameras and the national database to detect vehicles with no current mot. The system flags non-compliant vehicles in real time, even without a roadside stop. Vehicles with overdue MOTs have a higher failure rate when they do finally arrive for testing, suggesting that delays allow faults to worsen.

Driving without a valid mot can result in a £1,000 fine, and driving a dangerously defective vehicle can carry penalties of up to £2,500 plus three penalty points. Missing an mot can invalidate your vehicle insurance, leaving you personally liable for any accident costs. There is no grace period after an mot expiration - the moment the certificate expires, driving on public roads becomes illegal.

Annual mot tests check critical components like brakes, lights, tyres, steering, and emissions. MOT checks are crucial for vehicle safety and roadworthiness, and the test exists specifically to reduce accidents caused by preventable mechanical failures.

How MOT reminders reduce risk and hassle

MOT reminders help ensure vehicles are checked on time to detect faults before they become dangerous. Automated alerts give enough time to fix minor issues like worn tyres, cracked windscreens, or failed bulbs before the test.

By booking early, drivers can choose appointment slots that fit their schedule, rather than rushing to find a same-day test at whatever garage has space. Consistent use of mot reminders helps maintain a continuous record of mot compliance - useful when selling the vehicle or managing a business fleet.

Combine reminders with regular basic checks - tyre tread depth, lights, fluid levels - and you end up with safer, more reliable vehicles all year round. Relying on memory alone is risky, especially for households or businesses with multiple vehicles and busy diaries. A pay-nothing reminder service removes that risk entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I automatically get an MOT reminder when I buy a car?

No. MOT reminders are not sent automatically to every keeper. The driver must actively sign up using the vehicle's registration number and their own contact details. When buying a used car, do not rely on the previous keeper's reminders - register for your own alerts as soon as possible. Some garages send their own reminders to previous customers, but these are separate from official or independent reminder services.

Can I get reminders if my vehicle is on SORN or doesn't need an MOT?

Vehicles declared off the road (SORN) or those exempt from mot testing generally do not receive standard mot reminders. If the vehicle is brought back into use, check whether an mot is required and sign up for reminders before driving it on the road. Classic vehicles over a certain age may be mot exempt, but owners are still responsible for ensuring they are safe and roadworthy.

What happens if I change my email address or mobile number?

Log in to your mot reminder service and update your contact details as soon as you change phone or email provider. If you cannot access the old account, you can usually create a new reminder subscription from scratch using the registration number and new contact information. Double-check that test dates and mot status look correct after updating to ensure the right vehicle is still being tracked.

Can I use MOT reminders for a leased or company car?

In many cases, drivers of leased or company cars can set up mot reminders, but responsibility for booking the test may rest with the leasing company or fleet department. Check the lease agreement or company policy to see who should receive reminders and who must arrange the mot. Having personal reminders can still be helpful even if another team ultimately books the appointment.

Are email reminders safer than text messages for MOT alerts?

Both email reminders and sms messages can be effective, but the best option depends on how the driver normally checks messages. Use both channels where possible so that if one is missed or filtered, the other still reaches the driver in time. Keep spam filters, inbox rules, and phone notification settings configured so that mot reminders are not silently hidden.
