Post Offices in Glasgow: Branches, Opening Times and Nearby Services

Whether you need to post a parcel, pick up foreign currency or pay a bill, knowing where to find a post office in Glasgow can save you a frustrating trip. This guide covers the full network of post office branches across the city and its surrounding towns, including opening hours, services, in-store options and area-by-area coverage.

Key Takeaways

  • There are 91 post office branches in Glasgow and surrounding areas as of 2026, spanning from the West End and Maryhill to East Kilbride and Cumbernauld.

  • No post offices exist in Glasgow's central postcodes G1 to G5, meaning you will need to travel to branches in the West End, Maryhill or the south side for counter services.

  • Post offices in Glasgow typically open at 9am on weekdays and most close between 5pm and 5:30pm, with 81 branches open on Saturdays and 28 open on Sundays.

  • Many branches now sit inside supermarkets and convenience stores (such as Tesco Craigmarloch and Morrisons Cathkin), often offering longer evening and weekend hours.

  • Always use an online branch finder or postcode search before travelling, as hours and services vary significantly from branch to branch.

Overview of Post Offices in Glasgow

Glasgow's postal network includes roughly 91 post office branches spread across the city and nearby towns, covering everything from small counters in local shops to larger dedicated offices in areas like Easterhouse, Maryhill and East Kilbride. These branches handle everyday postal services such as letters, parcels and returns, alongside bill payments, some financial services and travel-related products.

Post office networks play essential social and economic roles in Glasgow's communities, providing access to mail services and government services for residents who may not have easy alternatives. Central Glasgow's lack of a branch in the G1–G5 postcodes is notable, but residential districts around the city enjoy strong coverage.

The main types of Glasgow branches include:

  • City and suburban branches - standalone counters on high streets and shopping parades

  • In-store branches - counters inside supermarkets and convenience chains

  • Rural and outreach branches - mobile or part-time services in semi-rural areas like Strathblane

  • Drop and collect points - smaller locations handling pre-paid parcel drop-offs

Glasgow City Centre and Main Branch Information

Unlike many UK cities, Glasgow currently has no standard post office branch in the core G1–G5 city-centre postcodes. Major shopping streets such as Buchanan Street, Argyle Street and Hope Street are all without a Post Office counter. The nearest branches are approximately 3.5–4 km from Glasgow Central Station.

The frequently used "main" branches that most closely serve the city centre include: the West End Post Office on Great Western Road (the closest substantial office to Glasgow Central Station), Maryhill Post Office serving the north of the city, and Battlefield Road and Newlands–Cathcart area branches serving the south side.

There is a historic Glasgow G1 address at 136 West Nile Street (G1 2RD) that appears in some local business directories. However, this is not a standard high-street post office counter for walk-in customers in the way most people expect, and its service range and hours are limited.

If you are heading into the city centre, plan ahead. Use an online branch finder to identify the closest branch to Glasgow Central or Queen Street stations before you travel.

Buying Postage Online and Dropping Off at Glasgow Branches

Glasgow customers can buy postage online through Royal Mail, Evri, DPD and similar carriers, often from around £2.90–£3.00 for small parcels. The process is straightforward: weigh and measure the parcel at home, buy and print the label online, then drop it at a participating Glasgow post office branch.

Buying postage online speeds up visits at busy branches like the West End or Easterhouse. You can drop pre-labelled returns at Pollok, Maryhill or East Kilbride branches without queuing for counter service. Always check which Glasgow post offices accept your chosen carrier before purchasing postage online.

Delivery Offices and Depots Serving Glasgow

It is important to understand the difference between customer-facing post office branches and operational delivery offices that handle mail sorting for Glasgow. Delivery offices are run by royal mail group ltd and are not the same as retail post office counters.

Key glasgow royal mail group facilities include: Glasgow G41–42–45 Delivery Office at 86 Victoria Road handling mail for south-side postcodes, Glasgow South East Delivery Office at 14 Fullarton Drive (G32 8FA) covering east and south-east districts, and a major logistics depot at 100 Cambuslang Road in Cambuslang Investment Park (G32 8NB) supporting high-volume distribution.

Some delivery offices allow customer parcel collection during limited hours. Check your "Something for you" card or online tracking for the precise collection point. These sites typically do not offer the full range of retail counter services such as banking or travel money.

FAQ: Post Offices in Glasgow

Which Post Office is closest to Glasgow Central and Queen Street stations?

There is no post office inside either Glasgow Central Station or Glasgow Queen Street, and none in the core G1–G5 shopping streets. The West End branch is the closest to the city centre, located on Great Western Road approximately 3.8 km away. Maryhill Post Office to the north and Battlefield Road branches to the south are also within a short bus or subway journey. Use a postcode search from the station to find the best branch for your route.

Are any Glasgow Post Offices open late in the evening?

While most Glasgow branches close by 17:30, there are around two dozen locations that open past 18:00 on at least one weekday. Late-opening branches are typically inside supermarkets and convenience stores such as Tesco Craigmarloch, Nisa Nitshill or Morrisons Cathkin. Filter for "open now" or "late opening" on your branch-finder tool. Service choice may be more limited in the evening, focusing mainly on posting and collecting parcels.

Can I collect missed parcels from any Post Office in Glasgow?

Missed parcels are normally sent to a specific delivery office or nominated collection point, not to every local post office counter. The Royal Mail card left at your address will state exactly where and when the parcel can be collected, which may be a delivery office like the one on Victoria Road or Fullarton Drive. Some services allow redirection to a participating branch, but this must be arranged online in advance. Always check tracking information before heading out.

Do Glasgow Post Offices offer Sunday or bank holiday services?

A minority of Glasgow post offices open on Sundays - specifically, 28 post office branches in Glasgow open on Sundays - typically those housed inside supermarkets or larger convenience stores. Sunday hours are shorter than weekdays and mainly cover basic mail and parcel transactions. On UK bank holidays, most standalone branches are closed, while some in-store counters may open with restricted Sunday-style hours. Check individual branch listings before travelling.

How can I check if a specific Glasgow Post Office is open right now?

The quickest way is to use an online branch finder, searching by postcode or branch name. The results will display today's opening times and whether the branch is currently open. Many tools also flag special hours for Saturdays, Sundays and bank holidays, plus temporary closures. Bookmark your nearest Glasgow branches for easy reference, and call ahead if travelling a long distance since hours can change at short notice.
