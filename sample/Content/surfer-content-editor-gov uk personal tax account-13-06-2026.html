GOV.UK personal tax account: how to sign in, set up and use it

Your gov uk personal tax account is the fastest way to see what HMRC knows about your earnings, check whether your tax code is right and claim money you are owed-all without picking up the phone. This guide walks you through every step, from creating your account to using it day to day during the 2026–27 tax year.

Key Takeaways

  • A GOV.UK personal tax account is a free, secure online service from HM Revenue and Customs where UK taxpayers can view and manage their income tax, national insurance and other personal tax details around the clock.

  • To use the personal tax account in 2026 you need to sign in with either a government gateway user id and password or the newer GOV.UK One Login. Photo ID may be required to prove your identity during setup.

  • Once inside, you can check tax codes, estimate current-year income tax for 6 April 2026 to 5 April 2027, track PAYE details and claim certain tax refunds directly from your account or via the hmrc app.

  • People who cannot create a personal tax account can still contact HMRC or use online calculators to estimate their income tax and potential tax refund without signing in.

  • The sections below give concise, step-by-step instructions with bullet points so you can get set up quickly and avoid common issues.

What is a GOV.UK personal tax account?

A Personal Tax Account (PTA) is a secure online portal provided by HMRC on GOV.UK. Think of it as a digital filing cabinet for your tax affairs-not a bank account, but a single place to see and manage everything HMRC holds about your personal tax position. The service is run by HM Revenue and Customs and pulls information from PAYE employers, pension providers and self assessment records where relevant. It lets UK taxpayers view and manage tax details without contacting HMRC by phone or post.

What you can see inside:

  • Your tax code and how it has been calculated

  • Estimated income tax for the current tax year

  • Your national insurance record and any gaps in contributions

  • A state pension forecast (the full new State Pension is around £230.25 per week in 2026–27)

  • Open tax refund claims and Marriage Allowance status

  • Self assessment notices, deadlines and payments history

The service is free to use, available 24/7 and entirely separate from business tax accounts used for VAT, Corporation Tax or CIS.

Who can use the personal tax account?

Most UK residents who pay income tax or national insurance can use the service. Common eligible groups include:

  • Employees paid through PAYE

  • Pensioners drawing private or workplace pensions

  • Self assessment taxpayers with untaxed income such as rent or dividends

  • People claiming HMRC-administered benefits or allowances like Child Benefit or Marriage Allowance

You generally need a national insurance number and a UK address to register. People living abroad with UK income can sometimes access the account, but certain online features may be limited. If you cannot pass the online identity checks, HMRC may offer verification by post or phone before you can start using your personal tax account.

How to sign in or set up your personal tax account

There are two sign-in methods available in 2026. You can log into PTA using a Government Gateway ID or other digital identification methods such as GOV.UK One Login. Always go directly to GOV.UK and search "personal tax account" rather than following links from emails or texts-this is the simplest way to avoid scams.

Typical sign-in sequence:

  • Visit the personal tax account page on GOV.UK

  • Choose "Sign in" or "Create sign in details"

  • Enter your email and password

  • Complete two-factor authentication (SMS code or authenticator app)

  • Confirm your identity using documents and your national insurance number

You need sign in details to create a personal tax account. Make sure your web browser accepts cookies before you begin. Once set up, the same credentials work for related HMRC services such as checking current-year income tax or viewing national insurance records.

If you already use HMRC online services

Existing users with a government gateway user ID and password should continue using those details to access their personal tax account. Your login may already be linked to both your personal tax account and any business tax account, so keep the credentials safe.

Check that your contact details, email and mobile number are up to date so you receive security codes and HMRC alerts. If you forget your government gateway details, the online recovery process will ask for your national insurance number and answers to security questions.

If you have never used HMRC online services

Choose "Create sign in details" on the GOV.UK personal tax account page. Even if you already use GOV.UK One Login for other services, you may still need to link it to HMRC by answering tax-related identity questions.

Standard identity evidence accepted in 2026 includes:

  • UK passport or photocard driving licence

  • Recent payslips or P60

  • Details from a UK credit reference file

Proving your identity may require photo ID like a passport. The setup process involves verifying identity through a national insurance number and identity documents. Setting up a personal tax account is free and typically takes only a few minutes-have your documents ready before you start. Once created, the same login will be used every time you access your account or the hmrc app.

What you can do in your personal tax account

Once signed in, you can:

  • View and update personal details like address, name changes and contact information so HMRC letters and tax codes stay accurate

  • Check PAYE employment and pension details, including income received so far this tax year and tax deducted by each employer

  • See your current tax code, read what each element means and correct information causing an incorrect code

  • Track your national insurance record, identify gaps and understand how they affect your future state pension

  • Start or track certain tax refund claims, such as overpaid PAYE tax or uniform expenses

  • Link to your online self assessment tax return, view past returns and check upcoming payment deadlines

  • Opt for electronic communication instead of paper letters

Users can also check tax codes and view tax estimates through PTA at any time.

Checking your Income Tax and tax code

You need to sign in to check your income tax. The personal tax account shows an estimate of your total income tax for the current tax year-6 April 2026 to 5 April 2027-based on PAYE data HMRC holds from employers and pension providers.

The UK applies income tax rates across several bands: a basic rate, higher rate and additional rate. These interact with the Personal Allowance (£12,570 for 2026–27, reflected by tax code 1257L for most taxpayers). Tax rules determine how allowances reduce the amount of income that gets taxed.

Inside your account you can:

  • See the tax code assigned to each job or pension

  • Read plain-English explanations of what each code element means

  • Spot problems such as emergency codes or codes that still reference an old employer

  • Update key information-expected pay, company car details or benefits in kind-so HMRC can recalculate your code within a few working days

If you are on self assessment only, current-year PAYE details may not appear here; rely on your self assessment calculations instead.

Tax refunds and overpayments

The personal tax account is often the quickest way to find out if HMRC owes you money. Common situations where a tax refund might be due include:

  • Changing jobs during the year

  • Being placed on an emergency tax code

  • Short-term or seasonal work

  • Overpaying tax on pensions

PTA allows users to handle claims and tax refunds directly into their bank account. You may see a P800 or PA302 calculation inside your account and be able to request the refund online. Never share login details or bank information via email or text-legitimate claims are processed only through GOV.UK or the hmrc app.

If a refund is not visible, use official GOV.UK forms or contact HMRC by phone or post. Keep records such as P45s, P60s and payslips. Refunds related to self assessment are normally dealt with through the online tax return system rather than the PAYE income viewer.

Self Assessment and your personal tax account

Some people must complete a self assessment tax return even if they also pay tax through PAYE. Common reasons include self-employment above the annual threshold, significant untaxed income such as property rents, high income affecting Child Benefit, or complex investment income.

PTA enables submission and tracking of self assessment tax returns. Inside your account you can see notices to file, deadlines, links to your online return and payments history. You can make payments towards your bill and check whether payments on account are due. If figures in the self assessment system and the PAYE viewer look inconsistent, review both and contact HMRC if you suspect an error.

Using the HMRC app with your personal tax account

The hmrc app for Android and iOS gives quick mobile access to many of the same features as the personal tax account on GOV.UK. The HMRC app can be used to check income tax, view national insurance contributions, see tax codes, store your national insurance number and track some tax refunds.

Sign in using your existing government gateway or GOV.UK One Login details-the same security checks apply. The app is particularly useful for checking estimated income tax for the current tax year on the go during 2026–27. Only download the official app from trusted app stores, checking the publisher is "HM Revenue & Customs".

If you cannot create or access a personal tax account

Not everyone will pass HMRC's online identity checks. If verification fails, you may be offered alternative methods such as sending documents by post or confirming identity over the phone. Contact HMRC if you cannot create a personal tax account-they can help by phone, post or through trusted agents and charities authorised to act on your behalf.

You can still estimate your income tax without signing in by using HMRC's online calculators on GOV.UK. Keep copies of payslips, P45s, P60s and tax letters, as these will be needed if you speak to HMRC without a personal tax account. Check accessibility support on GOV.UK if you have disabilities or language needs that make online services difficult to use.

Dealing securely with HM Revenue and Customs online

Staying safe while using your personal tax account matters. Key advice:

  • HMRC will never ask for full passwords, PINs or bank details in an email, text or social media message

  • Always access your account by typing "gov.uk" into your browser or using the official hmrc app-never click links in unexpected messages

  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication

  • Keep your operating system and browser up to date

  • Report suspicious emails, calls or texts using the details on GOV.UK

The HMRC Charter sets out what taxpayers can expect from HM Revenue and Customs, and what customs expects in return, including honesty and keeping records up to date.

FAQs about the GOV.UK personal tax account

Do I need a personal tax account if I only have one PAYE job?

It is not compulsory, but strongly recommended. Even with a single PAYE job, a personal tax account lets you check your tax code, income tax estimates and national insurance record so you can spot errors before they affect your pay.

How often is my personal tax account updated?

PAYE information is normally updated after employers and pension providers submit payroll data to HMRC, which can take a few days. Figures may lag slightly behind real-time pay but should reflect the current tax year reasonably accurately.

Can I see previous years' Income Tax in my personal tax account?

The account primarily focuses on the current tax year, but it provides links to separate services showing how much income tax was paid in earlier years-for example, 6 April 2025 to 5 April 2026-along with downloadable letters and calculations where available.

Is the personal tax account the same as Self Assessment?

They are related but different. The personal tax account is a dashboard showing PAYE and other personal tax details, while self assessment is the annual process of filing a full tax return. Many self assessment users will see links to their returns from inside the personal tax account.

Can someone else manage my personal tax account for me?

A trusted agent, accountant or relative can be formally authorised with HMRC to deal with your taxation affairs, but you should never share your own login details. Instead, grant authority through official HMRC channels so the agent can use their own credentials to find and manage the relevant information on your behalf.
