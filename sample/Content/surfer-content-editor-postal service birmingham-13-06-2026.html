Postal Service Birmingham – Guide to Post Offices, Royal Mail and Parcelforce Worldwide

Whether you need to post a parcel, collect a missed delivery or handle everyday banking, Birmingham's postal network has you covered. This guide breaks down where to find post office branches, royal mail delivery offices and parcelforce worldwide depots across the city, so you can get in and out without wasting time.

Key Takeaways

  • Birmingham has 54 post office branches plus dedicated royal mail delivery offices and a parcelforce worldwide depot, providing broad coverage across the west midlands.

  • There is no traditional high-street post office branch directly on New Street or at victoria square, but nearby options on union street, in the Jewellery Quarter and at Witton on manor road fill the gap.

Overview of Postal Services in Birmingham

The postal service birmingham network is split across three distinct operations: post office branches handle retail counter services, royal mail delivery offices manage sorting and deliveries, and the parcelforce worldwide depot handles express parcels. As of mid-2026, there are 54 post office branches serving Birmingham and nearby districts such as Sutton Coldfield, Rubery and Wythall.

Branches range from full high-street offices to smaller counters inside convenience stores like Spar, One Stop and Premier shops. Birmingham's postcode area (B1–B98) stretches from the city centre through suburbs like northfield and Perry Barr out to edge-of-city locations near Redditch and Solihull. Post Office branches handle UK and international posting, Collect+ parcel services, bill payments and everyday banking with major UK banks.

Wythall, Drakes Cross Parade and South-Edge Communities

Post Offices in and Near Birmingham City Centre

Unlike some UK cities, Birmingham has relatively few post office counters inside the central B1–B5 area. Birmingham Post Office is located at 31-33 union street, B2 4SR. This branch opens monday to friday 09:00–18:00, saturday until 17:30 and sunday 11:00–17:00 - one of the few city-centre options with sunday trading. Corporation Street (118 Corporation Street, B4 6SX) is another option near pinfold street and the Bullring area.

There is no dedicated Post Office at Bullring itself or directly on New Street or victoria square, so plan a 10–15 minute walk to union street or the Jewellery Quarter. Branch details can change, so always confirm live information on the official branch finder before travelling.

Royal Mail in Birmingham – Delivery Offices and Mail Centre

Royal mail's principal hub is the Birmingham Delivery Offices and Mail Centre at St Stephens Street, Birmingham B6 4AB. This facility sorts and distributes letters and parcels across multiple B-postcodes. Standard letter deliveries and standard small parcel deliveries are typically made monday through saturday.

If you missed a delivery, check your "Something for you" card or track online to confirm whether your item is at St Stephens Street or a different local delivery office before making the trip. Bring photos ID and your card to collect. Birmingham also offers 24/7 royal mail drop-offs and Self-Service Drops available 24/7 across Birmingham for pre-paid items - useful for anyone needing to post outside normal hours.

Frequently Asked Questions about Postal Services in Birmingham

Is there a main Post Office for Birmingham, or just local branches?

Birmingham does not have a single "head" main Post Office serving all districts. Instead, it relies on a network of 54 branches spread across the city. For city-centre visitors, the branch at 31-33 Union Street or the Jewellery Quarter office on Vyse Street are the most practical. Royal Mail's Birmingham Delivery Offices on St Stephens Street handle mail processing, not retail services, so use local post offices for everyday posting and banking.

Where do I collect a Royal Mail parcel that could not be delivered?

Undelivered items are taken to the delivery office named on your "Something for you" card. In Birmingham this is often St Stephens Street (B6 4AB), but check the card and track online before travelling. Bring the card and photo ID. Delivery offices open early - often from 07:00 - but close earlier than standard post offices, so plan accordingly.

Are Birmingham post offices open on Sundays and bank holidays?

22 post office branches in Birmingham open on sundays, usually those inside supermarkets and convenience stores. Hours are typically limited to late morning and early afternoon. Most high-street branches and all royal mail delivery offices close on UK bank holidays. Use the branch finder to filter for sunday opening if weekend posting is essential.

What is the difference between Royal Mail, the Post Office and Parcelforce Worldwide?

Royal mail collects, transports and delivers letters and most parcels. The post office runs the retail counters where you buy stamps, send items and access financial services. Parcelforce worldwide is Royal Mail's express parcel brand with its own depot near Birmingham at Pavilion Drive (B6 7BB). Many Birmingham post offices accept Parcelforce parcels for drop-off, but specialist or large international shipments may require visiting the depot directly.

How can I find a late-opening or early-morning post office in Birmingham?

30 post office branches in Birmingham open after 6pm on weekdays - almost all of them are in-store counters inside supermarkets and convenience stores. Use the services near you tool and filter by opening time. Areas like Kings Heath, Chelmsley Wood and Perry Barr often have extended-hour counters. Always double-check closing times on the day you visit, as extended hours can change due to staffing or store refurbishments. TG Jones and similar local retail partners occasionally adjust schedules, so a quick search before you head out saves wasted journeys.
