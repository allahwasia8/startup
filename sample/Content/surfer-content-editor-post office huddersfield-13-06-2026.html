Overview of Post Office Services in Huddersfield

Huddersfield is a major town in West Yorkshire with a diverse population of residents, university students, and local businesses. Its postal network is built to handle high volumes, with around 26 post office locations across the area serving both the urban core and surrounding villages.

When people search for "post office huddersfield," they typically mean the main town-centre branch on New Street. However, delivery offices on Northumberland Street and at slaithwaite handle logistics, parcel collection, and missed deliveries separately.

Services across Huddersfield branches generally fall into four categories:

  • Postage and parcels - letters, tracked services, Parcelforce

  • Identity and licensing - passport Check & Send, DVLA renewals

  • Travel services - foreign currency, travel insurance

  • Financial and bill payments - cash withdrawals, deposits, mobile top-ups

Most central branches are open Monday to Saturday, with reduced or no service on Sundays and bank holidays. Always verify the latest hours online before travelling, as schedules can shift seasonally.

Other Key Huddersfield Postal Sites

Not every post office location in Huddersfield is a high-street counter branch. Royal mail delivery offices serve a different purpose - they're logistics hubs for sorting, delivering, and holding parcels for collection.

Huddersfield Delivery Office on Northumberland Street (HD1 1AA) is a major facility handling over 74,000 properties with approximately 210 staff across 140 delivery routes. It's where you'll head if you receive a "Something for you" card for a missed HD1 delivery. Opening hours are narrow: typically 08:00–10:00 Monday to Friday and 08:00–12:00 on Saturdays.

Slaithwaite Delivery Office at Spa Fields Industrial Estate (HD7 5AA) covers the western outskirts. Slaithwaite Delivery Office opens at 08:00 daily, closes at 10:00 from Monday to Friday, closes at 12:00 on Saturday, and the office is closed on Sundays.

Key differences from full-service branches:

  • Delivery offices do not offer travel money, banking, or licensing services

  • They handle parcel collection, prepaid drop-offs, and redelivery arrangements

  • Both tend to have more practical parking than the New Street branch, though Northumberland Street can get congested during peak collection times

Using Huddersfield Post Office for Sending and Receiving Post

Huddersfield post offices support everything from everyday letters to tracked business parcels and international shipping. Services at Huddersfield post office include UK and international postage across multiple tiers.

Posting options at the New Street branch include:

  • 1st and 2nd Class letters and parcels

  • Royal Mail Signed For and Special Delivery Guaranteed

  • International standard and tracked services

  • Parcelforce Express for heavier or time-sensitive shipments

For parcel collection after a missed delivery, royal mail leaves a "Something for you" card directing you to your local delivery office. Bring the card and photo ID to collect.

When posting time-sensitive items, note that last collection times may vary by 30 minutes depending on the service and branch. Special Delivery and international items typically have earlier cut-offs than standard domestic post. Business senders can also use bulk posting accounts and dedicated drop-off points across Huddersfield's retail and industrial areas.

Travel, Money and Everyday Services at Huddersfield Post Offices

Local Huddersfield post offices do far more than handle letters. The New Street branch acts as a convenient hub where you can:

  • Order foreign currency in advance or pick up common currencies on the spot

  • Purchase prepaid travel money cards and travel insurance

  • Pay utility bills, council tax, and top up mobile phones

  • Buy packaging materials, stationery, and greetings cards

Many Huddersfield residents use their local post office to manage finances without visiting a separate bank - particularly useful where local bank branches have closed. You can also protect valuable items during transit by purchasing insurance or opting for tracked, compensated services at the counter.

The exact range of services varies by branch, so check that your chosen location offers what you need before visiting.

Buying Postage Online and Dropping Off in Huddersfield

Buying postage online and dropping parcels off locally is one of the easiest ways to skip the queue. Weigh and measure your parcel at home, choose a service, print a label, and attach it securely.

After purchasing online, drop parcels at participating branches including the New Street post office (which offers Drop & Go) or at delivery offices like Northumberland Street and slaithwaite if your chosen service permits it.

Online postage is often cheaper than counter prices and can unlock extra tracking or compensation. Before heading out:

  • Ensure labels are legible and firmly attached

  • Remove any old barcodes or labels

  • Use robust packaging to protect contents and avoid delays

Tips for Visiting a Huddersfield Post Office

Central Huddersfield branches get busy at predictable times. A little planning goes a long way.

  • Best times to visit: Mid-morning or mid-afternoon on weekdays. Avoid lunchtime and the last hour before closing.

  • Prepare in advance: Complete customs forms for international parcels, address packages clearly, and check size and weight limits before you arrive.

  • Use online tools: Price finders and postage calculators can save time, especially for heavier or international items.

  • Self-service kiosks: Faster for simple letter and small parcel postage. Use the counter for complex, high-value, or customs-required items.

  • Payment: Cash, major debit and credit cards, and contactless payments are accepted at New Street. Bring photo ID if you need identity or licensing services.

FAQ – Post Office Huddersfield

What is the main Huddersfield Post Office address?

The principal town-centre post office is at 65–67 New Street, Huddersfield, West Yorkshire, HD1 2BQ. It sits in the HD1 postcode area close to major shops and bus routes.

Where do I collect a missed delivery in Huddersfield?

Royal mail "Something for you" cards typically direct HD1 customers to Huddersfield Delivery Office on Northumberland Street (HD1 1AA). Bring the card and photo ID. HD7 postcodes may be directed to the slaithwaite Delivery Office instead.

Do Huddersfield post offices open on Sundays?

Most traditional post office counters in Huddersfield, including New Street and all delivery offices, are closed on Sundays. Some agency branches inside supermarkets or convenience stores may offer limited Sunday hours - check individual listings before travelling.

Can I get foreign currency at Huddersfield Post Office branches?

Yes. The New Street branch offers travel money services and can order a wider range of currencies if you request them a few days in advance. Delivery offices do not provide this service.

Is parking available near Huddersfield post offices?

The New Street branch is best reached on foot or by public transport, though pay-and-display car parks like Queensgate are nearby. Delivery offices on Northumberland Street and at slaithwaite generally have more practical parking suited to quick parcel collection visits.
