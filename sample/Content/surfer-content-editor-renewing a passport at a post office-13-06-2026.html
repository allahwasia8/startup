Renewing a Passport at a Post Office: Step-by-Step Guide (UK 2026)

If you need to renew your uk passport before your next trip, doing it through a Post Office branch can save you from costly mistakes. This guide covers everything you need to know about renewing a passport at a post office in 2026, including current fees, what to bring, and how the process works for adult and child passports.

Key Takeaways

  • Most countries expect at least 6 months' validity remaining on your passport, and uk passports issued more than 10 years ago may no longer be valid for travel even if the printed expiry date says otherwise.

  • You can renew an adult passport or child's passport at selected post office branches using Digital Check and Send or Paper Check and Send.

  • Standard renewals typically take around 3 weeks once hm passport office receives a complete application. Post office services do not make processing faster-they reduce the chance of errors that cause delays.

  • Fees from April 2026: adult passport renewal costs £102.00 online or £115.50 by paper application. Child passport renewal costs £66.50 online or £80.00 by paper application. The post office charges an additional fee of £16 for either check and send service.

  • Digital Check and Send includes a compliant digital photo taken in branch. Paper Check and Send does not include photos-you must provide your own.

When and Why to Renew Your Passport Before Visiting the Post Office

Before booking any travel in 2026, check both your passport's expiry date and its issue date. Many destinations now require at least 6 months' validity remaining on your uk passport when you arrive, and EU/Schengen countries also enforce a rule that your passport must have been issued less than 10 years before your date of entry. Passports issued before September 2018 sometimes show extra months of validity beyond 10 years, but most border authorities no longer recognise those extra months.

You should consider renewing a passport if:

  • Fewer than 6 months remain before your passport expires

  • Your passport pages are full

  • Your current passport is significantly damaged

  • You have changed your name or your appearance has changed substantially

Both adult passport and child passport renewals follow similar rules, though fees and some evidence requirements differ.

Overview: Renewing a Passport at a Post Office vs Applying Online

You have three main routes for a passport renewal: apply online directly to hm passport office, use a post office check and send service, or post paper forms yourself.

  • If you apply online directly, it is usually the fastest and cheapest option-you pay only the hm passport office fee and handle your own digital photo.

  • Post office Check and Send is ideal if you want in-person help with a digital application or paper application, or you are unsure whether your form and photos meet requirements.

  • Sending a paper application by post yourself (without a post office check) is normally slower and more prone to delays if there are errors.

Post office staff check your passport application for obvious mistakes, but they do not make the passport decision. hm passport office always does that.

Post Office Passport Services Explained

Not all branches offer every passport service. Using the Post Office Branch Finder helps locate participating branches before you visit. The digital check send service is available at around 1,100 post office branches across the UK.

The two main services are:

  • Digital Check and Send - supports digital application completion in branch

  • Paper Check and Send - supports paper application checking and submission

Both services can be used for a new passport, renewing an adult passport or child's passport, and replacing lost or stolen passports. Many branches also offer a passport photo service, though customers can sometimes bring their own photos for paper applications if they meet hm passport office rules. Spring and summer are busier periods, so visiting earlier in the day or outside school holidays may reduce queues.

Digital Check and Send Service (Digital Application in Branch)

Digital Check and Send is a digital application completed in-branch on a post office system and submitted electronically to hm passport office. As of June 2026, Digital Check and Send costs £16.00 extra in addition to the hm passport office application fee. The fee includes a compliant digital photo taken in branch, a digital check of the application, and secure digital submission. Applicants typically do not need to provide printed photos when using this service. It supports adult passport and child's passport applications, including renewals, first passports, and replacements.

What to Bring for Digital Check and Send

What you need depends on whether you are renewing, applying for a first adult passport, or applying for a child.

  • Renewing or replacing: Bring your old passport (if available), proof of a name change if applicable (marriage certificate, deed poll), contact details for a person who can confirm your identity if the passport is lost or your appearance has changed significantly.

  • First adult passport: You will need your parent's details (names, dates and places of birth), their nationality, and an email address for someone who can confirm your identity (a countersignatory who meets hm passport office criteria).

  • Child's passport (under 16): Both parents' details are required for child passport renewal. You normally need details for all those with parental responsibility, plus the child's birth certificate and the old child passport for renewals. If you are the only parent named, bring supporting documents explaining why.

Post office staff cannot decide what evidence hm passport office will accept but will check that the basics are present.

Step-by-Step: Using Digital Check and Send in Branch

  1. Find a suitable branch offering digital check send using the branch finder. You must schedule an appointment to renew at a post office.

  2. Bring your documents, including your current passport and any supporting documents.

  3. A staff member will take a digital photo during the application process, checking it against biometric standards.

  4. Staff complete the digital application with you on their system, entering your details and checking for obvious mistakes. The digital check confirms mandatory fields are complete.

  5. You pay both the hm passport office fee and the £16 post office service fee by debit card or another accepted payment method.

  6. The application is submitted electronically. You receive a receipt with an application reference number that lets you track progress online.

Customers receive updates on their application progress via email or text at each stage. Most standard renewals take around 3 weeks, though times vary according to demand. Using Digital Check and Send does not guarantee a successful application-hm passport office may still ask for more information.

Paper Check and Send Service (Paper Application in Branch)

Paper Check and Send is designed for customers who prefer a paper application form or cannot use a digital application. The Paper Check and Send service costs £16.00 on top of the hm passport office passport fee, as of June 2026. You must bring your own photos that meet hm passport office rules; the post office fee does not include photographs for paper applications. You must provide two identical photos for paper applications. Staff will check your completed paper form, supporting documents, photos, and countersignature (if required) before sending the application by special delivery to hm passport office.

How to Make a Paper Application via the Post Office

  • Get the correct paper application form from a participating branch (adult, child, standard 34-page, or 50-page).

  • Complete the form at home in black ink, following hm passport office instructions. Obtain a countersignature if required.

  • Attach compliant printed passport photo prints meeting current rules (size, background, expression, no filters). One must be countersigned if hm passport office requires it.

  • At the branch, staff review the form, photos, and supporting documents. They highlight errors or missing sections and ask you to correct them.

  • Once everything is correct, the post office sends your application and original documents by Royal Mail special delivery to hm passport office, giving you a receipt and tracking number.

How and When to Pay

For Digital Check and Send, you usually pay both the hm passport office fee and the post office service fee in branch as part of the digital application process. For Paper Check and Send, the hm passport office fee is typically paid by card details or postal order attached to the form, while the post office service fee is paid at the counter. All fees must be paid in full before an application is submitted-incomplete or incorrect payment details can delay processing. Refunds of the post office service fee are only considered if hm passport office confirms a post office error directly caused a problem. Keep all receipts safely, as they contain your application reference and payment confirmation.

Adult vs Child Passport Applications at the Post Office

An adult passport (16 or over) is usually valid for 10 years, while a child's passport (under 16) is valid for 5 years. Children turning 16 may need to move to an adult passport depending on travel dates. Both adult and child passports renewals can often be done without a countersignature if the person looks similar to their last photo and their details have not changed-a countersignature is often not needed for child passport renewals in these cases.

First adult and first child passports, and some renewals where appearance has changed, may require a countersignatory to confirm identity, even when using Digital Check and Send. For child applications, the person with parental responsibility must attend or sign the form, and extra documents such as full birth certificates or legal orders may be required.

Proving a Child's Identity and Parental Responsibility

hm passport office commonly expects a UK long-form birth certificate, adoption certificate if relevant, and any previous child passport to prove a child's identity. If only one parent's details are available, or court orders affect parental responsibility, bring those documents. For some renewals (a child aged 12 or over renewing with a valid old passport), hm passport office may not require extra identity references. You need an email address for someone to confirm the child's identity. The adult making the application must have legal authority. Final identity checks are made by hm passport office, not the post office.

Photos for Your Passport Application

Passport photos are a common cause of delays. With Digital Check and Send, the post office normally takes a digital photo in branch that is automatically checked for size, lighting, and biometric standards. With paper applications, you must supply your own printed photos-the post office may offer a separate photo booth, but this is not included in the Paper Check and Send fee.

Key hm passport office passport photo rules for 2026:

  • Plain light background (cream or light grey)

  • Neutral expression, mouth closed

  • No head coverings except for religious or medical reasons

  • No sunglasses or tinted glasses

  • No digital filters or enhancements

  • A child passport renewal requires a new photo taken within a month

If you prefer to use your own photos for a digital application, make sure they follow official guidelines carefully before uploading.

Timings, Tracking and What Happens After You Apply

Digital Check and Send submissions are typically processed within three weeks. Standard passport renewal takes about 3 weeks once hm passport office receives a complete application, but times can stretch during peak periods. Online passport renewal is generally faster than paper applications.

Once submitted, hm passport office sends text or email updates at key stages: application received, being processed, approved, renewed passport printed, passport dispatched. You can track your application online using the reference on your receipt.

If you need a passport urgently within a few weeks, check current options such as the Fast Track service, which issues passports in 1 week after an appointment, and Online Premium services directly with hm passport office. Emergency passport services are available for urgent travel needs. The expedited passport renewal process allows up to 10 weeks as an outer bound for complicated cases. You can also renew your passport online if eligible, which may be quicker for straightforward renewals.

Common Problems and How the Post Office Can Help

Typical issues include incorrect paper forms, missing signatures, unsuitable photos, wrong fee amounts, unclear name changes, and damaged or stolen passports. Check and Send staff can spot obvious mistakes, ask you to correct sections before submission, and advise when you may need more supporting documents.

The post office cannot override hm passport office decisions, guarantee a passport will be granted, or speed up processing once the application has been submitted.

Bring more documentation than you think you need if there is any complication-multiple name changes, complex parental responsibility, or a photocopy of a lost document. If your new passport has not arrived in time, contact hm passport office directly rather than relying on the post office. Using the post office for passport renewal helps ensure applications are filled out correctly, but final decisions always rest with hm passport office. Staff verify documents and securely send applications to hm passport office on your behalf.

FAQs about Renewing a Passport at a Post Office

Can the Post Office speed up my passport renewal?

Post office services focus on checking and sending applications correctly. They do not control how fast hm passport office processes your passport. Urgent options like the 1-week Fast Track or Online Premium passport service are managed directly by hm passport office, though a post office may supply the paper forms needed to book some of these services. Applying at least 10 weeks before travel in busy periods is the safest approach.

Do I still need a countersignature if I use Check and Send?

Using Check and Send does not remove hm passport office rules about countersignatures. If your application type requires one, you must still provide it. This usually applies to first adult or child passports, some replacements for lost or stolen passports, and renewals when appearance has changed significantly. Post office staff will check the countersignature is complete but cannot act as your countersignatory.

Can I renew a passport that is already expired?

You can generally renew a uk passport after it has expired, but allow extra time in case hm passport office needs additional checks. If a passport has been expired for many years or is badly damaged, hm passport office may treat the application more like a first passport with extra questions. Bring as much identity evidence as possible to help staff ensure the application meets current guidance.

Can I change my name or personal details when renewing at the Post Office?

You can update details such as your name when renewing via Digital Check and Send or Paper Check and Send. You will need proof of a new name if applicable-typically a marriage certificate, civil partnership certificate, divorce decree absolute, or a deed poll. For some changes, hm passport office may request additional evidence after submission.

What if my passport is lost or stolen before renewal?

Report a lost or stolen passport to hm passport office as soon as possible, which can be done online or using forms available at the post office. You can then submit a replacement application using either Digital Check and Send or Paper Check and Send. Extra identity checks are usually required for lost or stolen passports, so bring extra evidence and expect the application process to take at least as long as a standard renewal, sometimes longer. Post offices offer Digital Check and Send for passport renewals including replacements, and renewing a passport by mail requires specific eligibility criteria in these situations. The post office logo on your receipt confirms your application was processed through an official channel.
